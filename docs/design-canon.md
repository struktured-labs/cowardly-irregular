# Cowardly Irregular: Master Design Canon (v0.3)

## 1. Project Identity

**Genre:** Retro-inspired JRPG with CRPG-level mechanical depth
**Visual Target:** 8-16-bit evolution aesthetic
**Core Influence Set:** Bravely Default, Chrono Trigger, EarthBound
**Engine:** Godot 4.3
**Design Goal:** "Build the JRPG that understands optimization culture without sacrificing narrative weight"

---

## 2. Core Vision

Cowardly Irregular targets system-aware gameplay centered on:
- Mastery
- Optimization
- Agency
- Automation versus engagement
- Cowardice (risk avoidance, system gaming)
- Irregularity (breaking intended constraints)

**Target Player Profile:**
- Min-maxers
- Intelligent autobattle users
- One-shot build engineers
- Mechanic exploiters
- Players seeking story cohesion

**Core Promise:** "The game never punishes intelligence. It responds to it."

---

## 3. Emotional & Meta Core

### Emotional Center

The narrative explores:
- Challenge preservation
- Player mastery exceeding system limits
- Tension between fun and dominance

**Gradual Revelations:**
- Difficulty is deliberately curated
- The antagonist maintains balance
- Systems adapt dynamically

### Final Confrontation Arc

- The villain acknowledges player superiority
- They concede inability to maintain challenge
- Post-game systems unlock upon completion

**Ending Variation Factors:**
- Job affinities
- Build philosophy
- Automation usage
- Risk behavior

---

## 4. The Final Antagonist

**Nature:** Not purely evil; a meta-aware curator

**Role Definition:**
- Designer of systems
- Keeper of difficulty
- Meta-aware curator
- Defender of challenge integrity

**Dynamic Response Triggers:**
- Volatility of player builds
- Risk appetite
- System manipulation patterns

**Final Outcome:** "The antagonist recognizes the player as the true master of the system and steps aside"

## 5. World Structure

The game progresses through phased overworlds, each affecting job evolution, sprite design, dialogue tone, mechanical framing, and narrative abstraction.

### Phase 1 — Medieval World
- **Tone:** Classic fantasy
- **Purpose:** Establish foundational jobs
- **Visual:** Traditional armor, robes, grounded pixel art
- **Zones:** Overworld, Cave Dungeon, Forest Dungeon, Harmonia Village

### Phase 2 — 90s Suburban / EarthBound-Inspired World
- **Tone:** Surreal modern nostalgia
- **Purpose:** Introduce economic and speculative systems
- **Visual:** Casual wear, early tech motifs

### Phase 3 — Steampunk / Industrial World
- **Tone:** Mechanical systems exposed
- **Purpose:** Automation becomes explicit
- **Visual:** Gears, steam, techno-arcane blending
- **Zones:** Steampunk Overworld (Cogsworth Junction), Industrial Overworld (The Efficiency District)

### Phase 4 — Abstract / Meta World
- **Tone:** Structural and philosophical
- **Purpose:** Reality and game logic destabilize
- **Visual:** Geometry distortion, symbolic motifs
- **Zones:** Futuristic Overworld (The Network), Abstract Overworld (The Remainder)

## 8. Core Base Jobs

### Mage (Base Caster)

**Identity:** Flexible magic user, neutral moral foundation, system experimenter
**Weapons:** Staves, Tomes

**Evolution Paths:**
- Wizard (Scholarly)
- Dark Mage (Morally ambiguous)
- Necromancer (Chaotic final evolution)
- Calculatory Archmage (Cold scholarly endgame branch)

#### Necromancer
- Highly destructive magic
- Can damage self and party
- May interact with save-state metaphysics
- Embodies chaotic system corruption

#### Calculatory Archmage Branch
- Precise casting, efficiency-driven, resource-optimized, emotionally cold

### Cleric (Starter Job)

**Canon Archetype:** Pious woman, pure but serious, quiet strength, deep faith, strong-willed
**Core Abilities:** White magic, healing, buffs, faith-scaling mechanics

**Evolution Concepts:**
- Devout Ascetic (Sacrifice equals power)
- Zealot (Power through suffering)
- Balanced Saint archetype

### Skiptrotter (Rogue/Ninja Hybrid)

**Identity:** Tactical coward, system exploiter, evasion-heavy, crit-focused, one-shot potential
**Personality Tilt:** Clever, slightly irreverent, risk-aware but indirect

### Speculator (90s World Job)

**Core Identity:** Risk interpreter, economic manipulator, volatility reader
**Speculator Inverter Variant:** Profits from collapse, reversal specialist
