# Job Evolutions by World

Jobs automatically shift when transitioning between worlds. These aren't unlocks — they're contextual transformations. Your Fighter doesn't "learn" to be a Security Guard; they simply *are* one in a world where swords become batons and armor becomes a uniform.

The party's abilities carry over but are re-skinned to fit the genre. A heal is still a heal, but the Cleric casts it with prayer in World 1 and hands you a first-aid kit in World 2.

## Evolution Table

| Base Job | W1 Medieval | W2 Suburban | W3 Steampunk | W4 Industrial | W5 Digital | W6 Abstract |
|----------|-------------|-------------|--------------|---------------|------------|-------------|
| Fighter | Knight-Errant | Security Guard | Steamknight | Enforcer | Firewall | Resolve |
| Cleric | Friar | School Nurse | Steampriest | Safety Officer | Antivirus | Faith |
| Mage | Hedge Wizard | Science Nerd | Alchemist | Lab Technician | Compiler | Logic |
| Rogue | Footpad | Skater Kid | Saboteur | Whistleblower | Exploit | Edge |
| Bard | Minstrel | Band Kid | Busker | PA Announcer | Broadcaster | Voice |

## How It Feels

The transformation happens during the world transition. It's not a menu. It's not a cutscene. Your sprite shifts, your ability names change, and someone in the new world calls you by your new title as though you've always been that.

**World 1 → 2 example (Fighter → Security Guard):**
The portal closes behind them. The sword is heavier than it was — or maybe the world is lighter. By the time they reach Maple Heights, the armor has become a navy uniform. The sword is a baton. The shield is a badge. Nothing changed, and everything changed. A kid on a bicycle says, "Hey, the mall security guy has a weird look on his face."

**World 3 → 4 example (Cleric → Safety Officer):**
The brass fittings fade. The vestments simplify into a hard hat and high-vis vest. The prayer beads become a first-aid kit. The holy text becomes an OSHA manual. The healing still works. The faith is... different. More regulatory than spiritual. "Apply pressure to the wound" carries the same weight as "May the light guide you," if you say it with enough conviction.

## Personality Drift

The job evolution also shifts dialogue tone:

- **Fighter → Security Guard**: Goes from "For honor!" to "Sir, I'm going to have to ask you to leave."
- **Cleric → School Nurse**: Goes from "The light protects" to "Have you been drinking enough water?"
- **Mage → Science Nerd**: Goes from "By the arcane arts!" to "Actually, that's thermodynamics."
- **Rogue → Skater Kid**: Goes from "The shadows conceal" to "Whatever, narc."
- **Bard → Band Kid**: Goes from "Hark, a ballad!" to practicing trumpet badly in the parking lot.

By World 6, the evolutions are reduced to their purest essence — Fighter becomes "Resolve" because that's what fighting always was. Cleric becomes "Faith" because that's what healing always required. The abstract world strips away the costume and shows you what the job *meant.*
