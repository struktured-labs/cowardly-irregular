# Job Evolutions by World

Jobs automatically shift when transitioning between worlds. These aren't unlocks — they're contextual transformations. Your Fighter doesn't "learn" to be a Security Guard; they simply *are* one in a world where swords become batons and armor becomes a uniform.

**World 1 is the exception.** In the medieval world, jobs keep their base names: Fighter, Cleric, Mage, Rogue, Bard. This is intentional — World 1 is the JRPG comfort zone. The names are generic because the characters are still archetypes, not yet individualized. They're tropes: the Fighter fights, the Mage casts, the Cleric heals. The genericness IS the design — it eases players into the combat system without asking them to also learn unfamiliar job titles.

The first evolution hits in World 2, and it's disorienting ON PURPOSE. You've been "Fighter" for an entire world. Now you're "Security Guard." The rename is part of the genre shock.

## World 1: The Trope Phase

In World 1, the characters ARE their class tropes. They haven't individualized yet. This is deliberate:

- **Fighter**: The one who hits things. Direct. Earnest. Doesn't overthink. "Point me at the problem."
- **Cleric**: The one who heals. Patient. Quiet faith. Doesn't need to be asked. Just does it.
- **Mage**: The one who studies. Curious. Slightly detached. Always has a theory.
- **Rogue**: The one who finds shortcuts. Pragmatic. Doesn't explain. Just does the thing.
- **Bard**: The one who talks. Observant. Can't stop narrating. Everyone's friend.

These are starting points, not destinations. By World 2, the genre shift and the job evolution start pulling them into more specific people. By World 6, they've shed the trope entirely and become their essence (Resolve, Faith, Logic, Edge, Voice).

The arc: **archetype → individual → essence**.

## How the First Evolution Feels

The transformation happens during the World 1 → 2 transition. It's the FIRST time it happens, and it's shocking — the party doesn't understand what's going on.

**Fighter → Security Guard:**
The portal closes behind them. The sword is heavier than it was — or maybe the world is lighter. By the time they reach Maple Heights, the armor has become a navy uniform. The sword is a baton. The shield is a badge. Nothing changed, and everything changed. A kid on a bicycle says, "Hey, the mall security guy has a weird look on his face."

The Fighter doesn't know what a "security guard" is. They just know they're still the one who stands in front. The title is different. The instinct is the same.

**Cleric → School Nurse:**
The vestments become scrubs. The holy symbol becomes a laminated badge. The prayer beads become antiseptic wipes. The Cleric still heals — but now it's called "first aid" and the faith feels different. Less cosmic, more practical. "May the light guide you" becomes "Have you been drinking enough water?" Same care. Different vocabulary.

**Later evolutions (World 3+):**
Each subsequent transition is less shocking because the party expects it now. But each one still changes something about HOW they think about their role. The Steamknight isn't just a Fighter with gears — they start thinking in mechanical precision. The Alchemist isn't just a Mage with vials — they start thinking in reactions and catalysts. The job evolution isn't cosmetic. It shifts the lens.

## Personality Drift

The job evolution also shifts dialogue tone:

**World 1 (generic, tropey):**
- **Fighter**: "I'll handle it." (Direct, no flourish)
- **Cleric**: "Let me help." (Simple, present)
- **Mage**: "Interesting." (Observing, not performing)
- **Rogue**: "I found a way." (Efficient, no explanation)
- **Bard**: "Let me tell you about—" (Always narrating)

**World 2 (first evolution shock):**
- **Fighter → Security Guard**: "Sir, I'm going to have to ask you to leave."
- **Cleric → School Nurse**: "Have you been drinking enough water?"
- **Mage → Science Nerd**: "Actually, that's thermodynamics."
- **Rogue → Skater Kid**: "Whatever, narc."
- **Bard → Band Kid**: *practices trumpet badly in parking lot*

By World 6, the evolutions are reduced to their purest essence — Fighter becomes "Resolve" because that's what fighting always was. Cleric becomes "Faith" because that's what healing always required. The abstract world strips away the costume and shows you what the job *meant.*
